Meet Heera Chandani

M.A. CCC-SLP/L

A Legacy of Compassion & Expertise

Heera Chandani, M.A. CCC-SLP/L, has been a practicing speech-language pathologist since 2006. She has worked in schools, therapeutic day programs, hospitals, private practices, and in home and community settings with several multidisciplinary teams.

She possesses a wealth of experience working with people of all ages, covering diverse neurological conditions, developmental disabilities, and disorders. Her areas of specialization include: apraxia of speech, autism spectrum disorder, language delay, ADHD, AAC, auditory processing disorder, executive functioning, cognitive communication disorders, social communication impairments, and bilingual evaluations (Urdu/Hindi-English).

In addition to therapy, Heera is committed to IEP (Individualized Education Program) and disability advocacy. She takes the time to understand each client's IEP and 504 plans, providing feedback to families and advocating for their needs in school and team meetings.

Global Vision & Teaching

Heera loves teaching and continues to guest lecture for graduate courses every year at Northwestern University. Grounded in a love of learning, she travels internationally to conduct workshops and present at conferences in locations such as India, Egypt, and the U.A.E. She has helped international schools and families access funding, build classrooms, and develop therapeutic programs.

Born in the U.S. and raised in Dubai, U.A.E., Heera is multilingual, being fluent and literate in English, Hindi/Urdu, and Sindhi, and literate in Arabic.

Personal Inspiration

Heera committed her life to service at age nine. She has volunteered at Mother Teresa’s Orphanage in India and helped set up special education programs in India and Africa.

She has been deeply shaped by her relationship with her younger brother, Dhiren, who has a developmental disability. Dhiren continues to be Heera’s greatest motivation. She lives in Chicago with him and, in her free time, enjoys traveling, photography, figure skating, yoga, and reading about neuroplasticity.

Our Profession

What is Speech Language Pathology?

Speech-language pathology is a profession that specializes in the diagnosis and treatment of communication impairments or disorders. This can include speech, language, cognitive, social pragmatic, and swallowing disorders. Speech-language pathologists, also known as speech therapists, specialize in evaluating and treating these deficits.

Our Mission

Since 2006, our mission is to improve the communication skills of our clients and positively affect their lives. The clinicians at Chandani & Burns, LLC recognize and respect the uniqueness of each child and adult. Clinicians utilize a personalized approach to ensure each client's speech and language treatment is effective and beneficial.

We view each individual as a gift to us, a blessing, a person we serve. We believe that each person has the right to be a productive and successful member of the community, to live a life with purpose, and to THRIVE with dignity and respect in an environment of change and challenge. We EMPOWER individuals and their families by:

  • Improving self-awareness and self-advocacy skills.
  • Providing skilled, effective, functional, collaborative, and individualized patient-centered therapy, resources and support to more fully attain their potential.
  • Providing education and advocacy to increase understanding, awareness, and supports for individuals with communication difficulties.
  • Serving families from diverse backgrounds in a culturally sensitive and appropriate manner - We strive to reflect the world in which we live in terms of diversity; we strive to embrace and respect ALL our clients, irrespective of their cultural, racial, and socio-economic backgrounds. In fact, we take into account the client’s cultural perspective and other language and culture influences on communication and pragmatics, during the therapeutic process.
  • Providing educational opportunities, including workshops and trainings for professionals and families around the globe.

About the Practice

Chandani & Burns, LLC is an SLP-owned and operated boutique style clinic in Skokie, IL. We provide clinic- and community-based, and in-school and in-home speech-language and occupational therapy services to children and adults.

Our clinicians specialize in autism, social pragmatics, apraxia, AAC, ADHD, executive functioning, cognitive impairments, and aphasia. We are known for our work with individuals with apraxia and complex communication needs.
